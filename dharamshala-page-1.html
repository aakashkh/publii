Chasing the Mountain Air: A Whirlwind Trip from Gurgaon to Dharamshala & Beyond!

Leaving behind the hustle of Gurgaon on the night of April 30th, our adventure began with a cozy bus ride courtesy of Laxmi Holidays from Kashmere Gate at 10:00 PM. After a comfortable overnight journey, we were greeted by the crisp morning air of Dharamshala around 9:00 AM the next day.

Home Away From Home (Almost!):
Thehr & Our Cozy Airbnb (May 1st - 5th)
An offbeat experience - stay at
Kareri Village : Zostel Plus (May 5th - 6th)

 

Instead of staying right in the heart of Dharamshala, we opted for a more tranquil retreat in Thehr, a quaint spot just about 6 kilometers away. Our Airbnb for the first four nights was a gem. It offered a perfect blend of peaceful surroundings and easy access to the main attractions. For last day we decided to stay at a nearby village Kareri in Zostel Plus.

Day 1: Manager's Treat, Missed Trails, and Mountain Vibes (May 1st)

Our first day kicked off with a warm invitation to my manager's house for a delightful lunch – a truly local experience! Post that, we were eager to explore and set our sights on the famed Bathu ki Ladi. However, as the evening approached, the road conditions deteriorated, and we decided against venturing further for safety reasons. A valuable lesson learned – mountain roads can be unpredictable! Despite not reaching our intended destination, the scenic drive and the anticipation of adventure filled our spirits.



Day 2: Mcleodganj Magic - Waterfalls, Temples, and a Bird's Eye View (May 2nd)

Day two was all about immersing ourselves in the vibrant energy of Mcleodganj. We took a scenic route via Naddi, soaking in the breathtaking views of the Dhauladhar range. Our first stop was the refreshing Bhagsu Nag Waterfall , a popular spot buzzing with energy.

Later in the day, we explored the bustling streets of Mcleodganj, paid our respects at the serene Dalai Lama Temple and experienced the thrill of the Mcleodganj Ropeway , which offered stunning panoramic views of the valley. The vibrant atmosphere of Mcleodganj in the evening is something truly special!

Day 3: Temples, Tea Estates, and Tranquil Gardens (May 3rd)

This day was dedicated to exploring the cultural and natural beauty around Dharamshala. We started with a visit to the revered Chamunda Devi Temple , a place filled with spiritual vibes. Our journey then took us to Palampur, a charming town known for its tea gardens.

A highlight of the day was a visit to the lush Wah Tea Estate, where we wandered through the verdant tea bushes and learned about the tea-making process. We also paid a visit to the ancient Baijnath Temple  steeped in history and architectural beauty. Our day concluded with a peaceful stroll through the Saurabh Van Vihar , a lovely park offering a serene escape.

Day 4: Seeking Blessings and Honoring Heroes (May 4th)

We continued our exploration of local temples with visits to the Anjanhar Mahadev Temple , known for its tranquil ambiance and the IndruNag Temple , perched amidst scenic surroundings. In the afternoon, we visited the poignant Dharamshala War Memorial, a place to reflect and honor the brave soldiers. The evening found us back in the lively Mcleodganj, exploring the Mall Road and indulging in some local shopping.

Day 5: A Scenic Ride to Kareri and Hostel Life (May 5th)

It was time for a change of scenery! Three couples of us booked two cabs for a comfortable ride to Zostel Plus Kareri , nestled amidst the stunning Kareri landscape. The two-way cab cost us ₹3000 per cab, split amongst us. The journey itself was picturesque, offering glimpses of the raw beauty of the Himalayas. Zostel Plus Kareri offered a different vibe – a backpacker's haven with stunning views and a chance to connect with fellow travelers.

Day 6: Nature's Pool and Valley Views (May 6th)

Our final full day was spent exploring the natural wonders around Kareri. We returned back to Dharamshala and hired a cab for ₹1000 and ventured to the refreshing Nature Pool Cafe – a unique spot to relax amidst nature. From there, we explored the captivating Kharota Valley , known for its scenic trails and tranquil atmosphere. It was the perfect way to soak in the last moments of our mountain retreat.


Homeward Bound (May 6th)
With hearts full of memories, we boarded our Laxmi Holidays bus from Dharamshala at 8:00 PM on May 6th. The overnight journey brought us back to the familiar chaos of Delhi around 6:00 AM the next morning.

Getting Around: For the initial days in Thehr and exploring nearby areas, we found renting a bike from a local rental service to be a convenient and fun way to get around. For longer distances and travel to Kareri, hiring cabs was the most comfortable option for our group.
Food, Contacts, and More :
This is just a snapshot of our incredible journey! I'll be adding more details about the delicious food we savored, the friendly locals we met (including contact names!), and perhaps some funny anecdotes along the way. Stay tuned for more updates!
In Conclusion:
This trip to Dharamshala and its surrounding areas was a refreshing escape from the city life. From spiritual experiences to adventurous explorations and breathtaking natural beauty, it had something for everyone. If you're looking for a blend of tranquility and adventure, this region should definitely be on your travel list!
